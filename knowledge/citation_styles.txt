# Citation Styles and Formatting Guidelines

## APA Style (American Psychological Association)
**In-text citations:**
- Single author: (Smith, 2024)
- Two authors: (Smith & Johnson, 2024)
- Three or more: (Smith et al., 2024)
- Direct quote: (Smith, 2024, p. 45)

**Reference format:**
- Journal article: Smith, J., & Johnson, A. (2024). Title of article. Journal Name, 25(3), 123-145. https://doi.org/10.xxxx
- Book: Smith, J. (2024). Title of book. Publisher.
- Website: Smith, J. (2024). Title of page. Site Name. https://www.example.com

## IEEE Style (Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers)
**In-text citations:**
- [1], [2], [3] (numbered sequentially)

**Reference format:**
- [1] J. Smith and A. Johnson, "Title of paper," Journal Name, vol. 25, no. 3, pp. 123-145, 2024.
- [2] J. Smith, Title of Book, Publisher, 2024.
- [3] J. Smith, "Title of webpage," Site Name, 2024. [Online]. Available: https://www.example.com

## MLA Style (Modern Language Association)
**In-text citations:**
- (Smith 45)
- (Smith and Johnson 123)

**Reference format:**
- Smith, John. "Title of Article." Journal Name, vol. 25, no. 3, 2024, pp. 123-145.
- Smith, John. Title of Book. Publisher, 2024.

## Vancouver Style (Numeric)
**In-text citations:**
- (1), (2), (3) (numbered by order of appearance)

**Reference format:**
- 1. Smith J, Johnson A. Title of article. Journal Name. 2024;25(3):123-145.
- 2. Smith J. Title of book. Publisher; 2024.

## Scientific Document Citation Best Practices
- **Consistency**: Use one style throughout the entire document
- **Completeness**: Include all necessary information (author, year, title, source)
- **Accuracy**: Verify all citation details are correct
- **Accessibility**: Ensure sources are accessible to readers
- **Current**: Prefer recent sources (within last 5-10 years when possible)

## Common Citation Errors to Avoid
- Missing author names or incomplete author lists
- Incorrect publication years
- Incomplete or incorrect journal titles
- Missing page numbers for articles
- Broken or incorrect URLs/DOIs
- Inconsistent formatting within the same document

## Digital Object Identifiers (DOIs)
- Preferred over URLs for academic sources
- Format: https://doi.org/10.xxxx
- Always include when available
- Provides permanent link to the source

## Reference Management Tips
- Number references in order of appearance
- Use hanging indentation for readability
- Alphabetize references if using author-date style
- Include all sources cited in the text
- Remove any sources not cited in the text
